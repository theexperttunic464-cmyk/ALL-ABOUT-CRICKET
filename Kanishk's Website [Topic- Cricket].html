Cricket Integrity

Protecting the Spirit of the Game

Types of Cricket Threats

  • Match-Fixing & Spot-Fixing: Deliberate manipulation of game outcomes or specific events like bowling no-balls or wide deliveries to favor betting syndicates. These actions damage the credibility of the sport and are often coordinated secretly.
  • Inside Information Abuse: Sharing team strategies, pitch reports, or player conditions with external parties to gain betting advantage. Even seemingly minor leaks can compromise matches.
  • Illegal Betting Syndicates: Organized groups influencing matches at domestic and international levels. They often pressure players or officials to manipulate results, sometimes using intimidation or financial incentives.
  • Counterfeit Gear: Fake bats, balls, and signed memorabilia sold online or at stadiums. Fans are scammed, and revenue losses can affect clubs and players.
  • Fake Social Media Accounts: Impersonating players, officials, or clubs to spread false information, solicit money, or influence betting outcomes.
  • Ticket Scams: Selling duplicate, fake, or overpriced tickets for matches, especially during major tournaments. Unsuspecting fans may lose money or face entry issues.
  • Doping & Substances: Using performance-enhancing drugs to gain an unfair advantage. Anti-doping violations can lead to bans, suspensions, and loss of trust.
  • Corrupt Player Agents: Agents who manipulate contracts, player transfers, or match participation for illegal profit. Players must verify credentials and report suspicious activities.
  • Cybersecurity Threats: Hacking team communications, accessing confidential emails, or manipulating digital scoring systems to influence betting or match decisions.

Real-World Cases (Expanded)

  • 2010 Pakistan Spot-Fixing: Three players were exposed for bowling intentional no-balls. The scandal resulted in bans and criminal convictions.
  • IPL Corruption (2013): High-profile players and team officials were banned in a major fixing scandal that reshaped anti-corruption rules in franchise cricket.
  • Shakib Al Hasan Ban (2019): One of the world's top all-rounders was banned after failing to report corrupt approaches, reinforcing strict reporting rules.
  • PSL Fixing Case (2017): Several players were suspended following investigations involving phone data, betting communications, and financial trails.
  • Global Fixing Networks: ICC worked with law enforcement to dismantle corruption rings targeting associate-level cricket and lower-income players.

Cricket Rules

  • Overs: Each team bowls a set number of overs per innings (e.g., 20 overs in T20, 50 in ODI).
  • Runs: Scored when batsmen successfully run between wickets or hit boundaries.
  • Dismissals: Ways a batsman can get out include bowled, caught, LBW, run out, stumped, etc.
  • Innings: Each team takes a turn batting and fielding. Some formats have one innings per team, others have two.
  • Fielding Restrictions: Powerplays and fielding limits exist in limited-overs cricket.
  • Result: The team with the most runs wins; ties and draws are possible depending on the format.

Team Formations

  • Batsmen: Openers, middle-order, and lower-order players who score runs.
  • Bowlers: Fast bowlers, medium pacers, and spinners who take wickets and restrict runs.
  • All-Rounders: Players skilled in both batting and bowling.
  • Wicket-Keeper: Specialist fielder behind the stumps.
  • Fielding Positions: Slip, gully, point, cover, midwicket, long-on, long-off, etc.
